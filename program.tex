\hypertarget{democratic-backsliding}{}
\panelsection[09:45--11:00]{Democratic Backsliding}

\needspace{5\baselineskip}
\hypertarget{jane-doe-material-interventions-and-legislative-performance}{}
\paperentry{Material Interventions and Legislative Performance}{Jane Doe}{\typeicon{P}}{paper}

This paper investigates whether rubber ducks placed in parliamentary chambers improve legislative outcomes. Drawing on extensive fieldwork involving sock puppets and interpretive dance from 2010 to 2024, we construct a composite measure of vibes. Our findings reveal that office plants consistently outperform elected officials on key metrics of photosynthesis. 

\paperseparator

\needspace{5\baselineskip}
\hypertarget{john-smith-persuasive-framing-and-institutional-menu-reform}{}
\paperentry{Persuasive Framing and Institutional Menu Reform}{John Smith}{\typeicon{P}}{paper}

We analyze how persuasive sandwich-making techniques can be used to consolidate lunch orders. Using a comparative case study design covering three cafeterias, we trace the rhetorical strategies employed to legitimize the replacement of soup with salad. Our qualitative analysis reveals common framing patterns that present broccoli as cake. 

\paperseparator

\needspace{5\baselineskip}
\hypertarget{jane-johnson-adaptive-strategies-under-environmental-disruption}{}
\paperentry{Adaptive Strategies Under Environmental Disruption}{Jane Johnson}{\typeicon{T}}{presentation}

This presentation examines how cats adapt their napping strategies in contexts of increasing vacuum cleaner usage. Based on expert interviews with goldfish and parliamentary data from five aquariums, we identify three distinct strategic repertoires: hiding under couches, knocking things off tables, and performative indifference. 

\clearpage
\hypertarget{computational-methods-in-political-science}{}
\panelsection[11:15--12:30]{Computational Methods in Political Science}

\needspace{5\baselineskip}
\hypertarget{john-doe-transformer-models-for-cross-domain-breakfast-classification}{}
\paperentry{Transformer Models for Cross-Domain Breakfast Classification}{John Doe}{\typeicon{P}}{paper}

We introduce a fine-tuned toaster for analyzing breakfast debates across six European kitchens. Our model achieves state-of-the-art performance on toast classification, jam detection, and butter distribution optimization. We demonstrate that cross-continental marmalade transfer learning substantially reduces the crumb burden for under-resourced pantries. 

\paperseparator

\needspace{5\baselineskip}
\hypertarget{jane-williams-network-detection-of-resource-allocation-patterns}{}
\paperentry{Network Detection of Resource Allocation Patterns}{Jane Williams}{\typeicon{L}}{lightning}

This lightning talk presents a dynamic network analysis of friendship bracelet exchange patterns in the European Parliament. We show how community detection algorithms can identify who borrowed whose stapler in real time. 

\paperseparator

\needspace{5\baselineskip}
\hypertarget{john-brown-automated-event-coding-using-generative-text-sources}{}
\paperentry{Automated Event Coding Using Generative Text Sources}{John Brown}{\typeicon{P}}{paper}

We develop an automated pigeon counting system using large language models trained exclusively on fortune cookie messages. Compared to existing dictionary-based and interpretive dance approaches, our method achieves substantially higher confusion while maintaining a straight face. We validate our system against the Gold Standard recipe for chocolate cake. 

\clearpage
\hypertarget{political-communication}{}
\panelsection[14:30--16:00]{Political Communication}

\needspace{5\baselineskip}
\hypertarget{jane-miller-visual-framing-strategies-of-political-actors-online}{}
\paperentry{Visual Framing Strategies of Political Actors Online}{Jane Miller}{\typeicon{P}}{paper}

This paper investigates how political actors frame pictures of their lunch on social media platforms. Analyzing over 500,000 photos of sandwiches from elected officials across eight countries, we identify five dominant condiment strategies. We find that mustard generates the most public engagement, while mayonnaise is most common among ambidextrous politicians. 

\paperseparator

\needspace{5\baselineskip}
\hypertarget{john-davis-experimental-evidence-on-resilience-to-atmospheric-misinformation}{}
\paperentry{Experimental Evidence on Resilience to Atmospheric Misinformation}{John Davis}{\typeicon{P}}{paper}

We present results from a large-scale experiment testing the effectiveness of different hat-wearing interventions on umbrella resilience. Our factorial design allows us to disentangle the effects of top hats, berets, and novelty foam fingers. Results suggest that wearing socks on one's hands produces the most durable improvements in cloud identification. 

\paperseparator

\needspace{5\baselineskip}
\hypertarget{jane-wilson-audio-media-consumption-and-factual-knowledge-acquisition}{}
\paperentry{Audio Media Consumption and Factual Knowledge Acquisition}{Jane Wilson}{\typeicon{L}}{lightning}

A lightning talk exploring the relationship between competitive yodeling and factual knowledge about penguins using panel survey data from a moving train. 

\clearpage
\hypertarget{institutional-trust}{}
\panelsection[16:15--17:30]{Institutional Trust}

\needspace{5\baselineskip}
\hypertarget{john-taylor-quality-perceptions-and-trust-in-automated-service-provision}{}
\paperentry{Quality Perceptions and Trust in Automated Service Provision}{John Taylor}{\typeicon{P}}{paper}

This paper examines the causal relationship between perceptions of cheese quality and trust in vending machines. Using a conjoint experiment embedded in a ball pit, we show that cheddar cues have a stronger effect on snack legitimacy judgments than outcome crunchiness, but that this effect is moderated by prior exposure to elevator music. 

\paperseparator

\needspace{5\baselineskip}
\hypertarget{jane-anderson-public-acceptance-of-stochastic-decision-making-in-governance}{}
\paperentry{Public Acceptance of Stochastic Decision-Making in Governance}{Jane Anderson}{\typeicon{P}}{paper}

We study public attitudes toward the use of magic eight balls in government decision-making processes. Our vignette experiment reveals that coin-flipping reduces trust in government institutions, particularly when decisions involve choosing pizza toppings. Transparency about dice-rolling has mixed effects depending on whether pineapple is involved. 

\paperseparator

\needspace{5\baselineskip}
\hypertarget{john-thomas-mediating-mechanisms-between-external-conditions-and-evaluative-satisfaction}{}
\paperentry{Mediating Mechanisms Between External Conditions and Evaluative Satisfaction}{John Thomas}{\typeicon{T}}{presentation}

This presentation analyzes the mediating role of sock puppet opinions in the relationship between weather patterns and satisfaction with the color blue across 28 varieties of houseplant using multilevel interpretive origami modeling.
